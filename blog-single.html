5 Warm-Up Mistakes You're Probably Making

January 15, 2026 · 5 min read

A good warm-up can make or break your session. It prepares your muscles, joints, and nervous system for the work ahead — yet most people either skip it entirely or do it wrong. Here are five of the most common warm-up mistakes and what to do instead.

1. Skipping it altogether

We get it — you're short on time and want to jump straight into the main workout. But diving into heavy lifts or high-intensity intervals without warming up increases your risk of injury and reduces performance. Even five minutes of targeted movement makes a difference.

2. Static stretching before training

Holding long stretches before exercise can actually reduce power output and muscle activation. Save static stretching for after your session. Instead, use dynamic movements like leg swings, arm circles, and walking lunges to increase blood flow and range of motion.

3. Doing the same warm-up every time

Your warm-up should match the session ahead. A lower-body strength day needs hip and ankle prep, while an upper-body session calls for shoulder and thoracic mobility. A generic five-minute jog on the treadmill won't cut it for every workout.

4. Rushing through it

A warm-up isn't just a box to tick. Moving too quickly through the motions means your body doesn't have time to respond. Slow down, focus on the quality of each movement, and give your joints time to open up.

5. Ignoring your weak spots

If you know your hips are tight or your shoulders are stiff, your warm-up is the perfect time to address it. Adding a few targeted activation exercises — like banded glute bridges or wall slides — can improve your movement quality for the entire session.

The takeaway

A warm-up doesn't need to be long, but it does need to be intentional. Match it to your session, move with purpose, and treat it as part of your training — not something separate from it.
